ENGLAND

  1. Don’t Call The Whole Of The UK “England"

    The United kingdom consists of 3 main parts:England, Scotland ,northern Ireland, so try not to mix that up.

  2. Do Not Use Scottish Money in England

    Banknotes issued by all members of the UK are legal currency and can be accepted throughout the UK. But it doesn't necessarily mean they will be.

  3. Standing on the Left, isn’t really a cool idea

    If there is a golden rule of London, it's this one. Do not, under any circumstances, stand on the left of an escalator in the London Underground. The left is for walking. The right is for standing. There is no debate. Failure to adhere to this will earn you unimpressed tuts from more seasoned commuters, and will instantly mark you as a tourist.

  4. Don’t Drive Down Oxford Street

    Being one of the world’s most famous shopping streets, pedestrians on Oxford Street tend to feel like they rule the road, which is fine, if you’re a pedestrian, but not so good if you’re behind the wheel of a car.

  5. They use Two Taps Instead Of One

    This one's easy to explain: We enjoy the performance of moving our hands very fast between the two horrific streams of water. We call it "tap dancing", and if you can do it well it proves you are truly British.

    - British citizen.

  6. There is something called a Mint Sauce.

    Yes that’s odd but I think it would be so cool if it becomes international thing

  7. Don’t Say Sorry

    If you want to apologize try to find other ways than sorry, i't's considered rude.

  8. You Can Drink On The Street

    You might be surprised to see people with drinks on the sidewalk. Not only is this allowed, but given the faintest hint of sun Brits will congregate on any patch of concrete, grass or pavement going.

  9. There Are Some Astonishingly Strange Place Names

    Don’t be surprised to find street names like Bottom Flash, Cock Alley, Booty Lane, Nether Wallop and Sandy Balls.Yes, really.

  10. In London, never make eye contact

    No matter how crowded the train or strange the situation, Londoners will steadfastly ignore eye contact at all costs. Striking up a conversation with a stranger on public transport is practically unheard of.
